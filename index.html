  • Let's face it, College isn't easy.

    Here are 4 ways you can help yourself build your self up.

      Tip #1: Donâ€™t Procrastinate or Cram

      Never wait until the day before an exam to try to learn all the material in a unit. It is a super ineffective way to process information and you will most likely forget a lot more of what you learned. Instead, space out your learning from day to day, giving your brain enough time to encode the information and giving you enough time to ask questions without getting last minute answers.

      Tip #2: Attend Class

      While many college students like to say that attending courses in college is optional, it has also been proven to save your time in many ways. Even though self-studying certain concepts has its own benefits, it is still overall much more efficient to just learn the material through attending lectures and taking notes since it can reduce your overall study time tremendously.

      Tip #3: Organize your Computer

      With so much on your plate as a university student, it is important to keep yourself organized. Since this generation is blessed with having technology, use it to your advantage! An organized laptop is super efficient and can help you save lots of time and effort keeping your agenda together. Make sure you organize your files and work system, and bookmark important tabs.

      Tip #4: Find Study Groups

      Having friends to study with can help students understand each other more than the internet and textbooks. A study buddy can help them understand their struggles and use their perspective to help them with questions.
